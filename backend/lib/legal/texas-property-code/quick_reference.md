# Texas Property Code Chapter 92 - Quick Reference

This guide provides fast lookup for common property management scenarios. For detailed legal text, search texas_property_code_chapter_92.txt.

## Security Deposits (Subchapter C, Sec. 92.101-92.109)

### Key Requirements
- **Refund deadline**: 30 days from surrender (Sec. 92.103)
- **Itemized deductions**: Required if any amount withheld (Sec. 92.104)
- **Forwarding address**: Tenant must provide; landlord must make good faith effort to return deposit (Sec. 92.107)
- **Bad faith retention**: Landlord liable for 3x deposit plus $100 + attorney fees (Sec. 92.109)
- **Normal wear and tear**: Cannot be deducted (Sec. 92.001(4))

### Common Scenarios
- **Advance notice requirement**: Only valid if underlined or bold in lease (Sec. 92.103(b))
- **Presumption of refund**: After 30 days without accounting, tenant presumed entitled to full refund (Sec. 92.104(c))
- **Written description required**: Must itemize deductions and provide receipts/estimates (Sec. 92.104(b))

## Repairs and Habitability (Subchapter B, Sec. 92.051-92.062)

### Landlord Duties (Sec. 92.052)
1. Make reasonable repairs/remedies to condition materially affecting physical health/safety
2. Applies only if tenant gives notice AND one of:
   - Landlord has notice defect exists
   - Landlord should have known through reasonable inspection
   - Landlord received government notice of condition

### Tenant Notice Requirements (Sec. 92.056)
**Written notice required specifying:**
- Problem/condition
- Building/portion affected  
- Remedial action requested

**Delivery methods:**
- Registered or certified mail, return receipt
- Hand delivery with signed receipt
- Electronic delivery per parties' agreement

### Tenant Remedies (Sec. 92.056 & 92.0561)

**If landlord doesn't remedy within reasonable time:**
1. **Terminate lease** - with proper notice
2. **Repair and deduct** - strict requirements (Sec. 92.0561):
   - Notice given, reasonable time passed
   - Tenant current on rent
   - Repairs cost ≤ one month's rent OR $500 (whichever greater)
   - Retain receipts
3. **Judicial relief** - actual damages, one month's rent + $500, attorney fees, court costs

## Retaliation (Subchapter H, Sec. 92.331-92.335)

### Prohibited Retaliatory Actions (Sec. 92.331)
Landlord may NOT retaliate by:
- Filing eviction
- Depriving tenant of use of premises
- Decreasing services
- Increasing rent
- Terminating lease

### Protected Tenant Actions
- Complaining to landlord about habitability
- Complaining to government agency
- Requesting repairs under Sec. 92.056
- Requesting landlord comply with law
- Tenant organizing/joining tenant union
- Exercising rights under Sec. 92.016-.0162 (family violence, sexual assault, stalking)

### Rebuttable Presumption (Sec. 92.332)
Retaliation presumed if action within 6 months of protected conduct (except rent increase if proper notice given before protected conduct)

## Entry and Access (Sec. 92.008, 92.0081)

### Landlord Entry Rights
- **No general right of entry** specified in statute
- **Cannot abuse right of access** (Sec. 92.008(a))
- **Emergency**: May enter during emergency without notice

### Utility Interruption (Sec. 92.008)
Landlord cannot interrupt utilities (water, gas, electric, AC, heat) to force tenant out except for:
- Bona fide repairs, construction, emergencies
- Periods of abandonment
- Protective action during extended absence
- Tenant requests in writing

**Penalties for violation:**
- Actual damages, one month's rent + $500, attorney fees, court costs (Sec. 92.008(c))
- Civil penalty $500-$1,000 per interruption (Sec. 92.008(d))

## Disclosure Requirements

### Ownership Disclosure (Subchapter E, Sec. 92.201)
Must disclose within 7 days of request or before lease if tenant requests:
- Owner's name and business address
- Management company name and address

### Other Required Disclosures
- **Flooding history** (Sec. 92.0135): If in 100-year floodplain
- **Parking/towing** (Sec. 92.0131): Vehicle towing and parking rules  
- **Emergency phone** (Sec. 92.020): For maintenance requests
- **Service of process agent** (Sec. 92.003): Name and address

## Security Devices (Subchapter D, Sec. 92.151-92.170)

### Landlord Duties (Sec. 92.151, 92.153, 92.154)
- **Keyless bolts**: Required on exterior doors
- **Window latches**: Required on windows
- **Door viewers/peepholes**: Required on exterior doors without window
- **Sliding doors**: Security bar/security pin required

### Rekeying (Sec. 92.156, 92.159, 92.164)
Tenant may request rekeying if:
- Roommate moves out (tenant pays)
- Family violence victim (landlord pays if protective order or police report)
- Sexual assault/abuse victim (landlord pays if conviction, deferred adjudication, or protective order)
- Tenant reports unauthorized entry (tenant pays unless landlord's fault)

**Timing**: Within 7 days of request

## Smoke Alarms (Subchapter F, Sec. 92.251-92.260)

### Requirements (Sec. 92.252, 92.253)
- Required in each bedroom
- Required outside each sleeping area
- Required on each story including basements
- Must be working at move-in
- Landlord installs/inspects; tenant maintains/tests

### Penalties (Sec. 92.260)
- Landlord failure: One month's rent + $100, attorney fees (no damages for temporary absence less than 24 hours)
- Tenant removal/disconnection: One month's rent + $100, plus actual damages to landlord

## Late Fees (Sec. 92.019)

### Requirements
- Only if in written lease
- Grace period: Cannot charge until after 2nd day rent due (e.g., if due 1st, can't charge until after 3rd)
- Reasonableness: Fee must be reasonable
- Initial notice only: If multiple months unpaid, can only charge late fee for initial month

## Family Violence / Sexual Assault Rights (Sec. 92.016, 92.0161, 92.0162)

### Early Lease Termination Rights
Tenant may terminate lease early without liability if:
- **Family violence victim** (Sec. 92.016): With protective order or dating violence protective order
- **Sexual assault/abuse victim** (Sec. 92.016): With protective order
- **Stalking victim** (Sec. 92.0162): With protective order or victim of stalking offense

### Notice Requirements
- Written notice to landlord
- Copy of protective order OR documentation specified in statute
- Move out by later of: 30 days after notice OR end of month following notice month

### Landlord Obligations
- May not disclose information related to termination unless required by law
- Must allow victim to remove belongings without interference

## Lockouts (Sec. 92.009)

### Prohibited
Landlord cannot intentionally prevent tenant from entering by:
- Changing door locks
- Adding or removing doors or windows  
- Other exclusion from premises

### Exceptions
- Pursuant to judicial writ
- Tenant abandoned premises (with proper procedures)

### Remedies for Tenant
- Actual damages, one month's rent + $500 (whichever greater)
- Attorney fees
- Court costs
- Injunctive relief

## Attorney Fees (Sec. 92.005)

**Prevailing party** entitled to reasonable attorney fees in actions under:
- Sec. 92.052 (repairs)
- Sec. 92.056 (tenant remedies)
- Subchapter C (security deposits)
- Subchapter D (security devices)
