# Texas Property Code Notice Templates

All templates must be customized for specific situations. Consult with legal counsel for compliance.

## 1. Security Deposit Accounting (Sec. 92.104)

**When to use**: Within 30 days of tenant surrender, if withholding any portion of security deposit

**Requirements**: 
- Itemized written description of deductions
- List of charges with supporting documentation (receipts, estimates, invoices)

---

### Template: Security Deposit Itemization

```
[Date]

[Tenant Name]
[Forwarding Address]
[City, State ZIP]

RE: Security Deposit Accounting for [Property Address]

Dear [Tenant Name]:

This letter provides the accounting of your security deposit for the property located at [Property Address]. You surrendered possession on [Surrender Date].

SECURITY DEPOSIT RECEIVED: $[Amount]

DEDUCTIONS:
1. [Description of damage/cleaning needed]
   Amount: $[Amount]
   [Attach: Receipt/invoice/estimate]

2. [Description of damage/cleaning needed]  
   Amount: $[Amount]
   [Attach: Receipt/invoice/estimate]

3. Unpaid rent for period [dates]
   Amount: $[Amount]

4. [Other deduction]
   Amount: $[Amount]
   [Attach: Receipt/invoice/estimate]

TOTAL DEDUCTIONS: $[Total]

REFUND AMOUNT: $[Deposit minus deductions]

Enclosed is a check in the amount of $[Refund Amount] / A check in the amount of $[Refund Amount] will be mailed separately.

[If no refund due]: The total deductions equal or exceed the security deposit amount. No refund is due.

Supporting documentation for all deductions is attached to this letter.

Sincerely,

[Landlord/Property Manager Name]
[Company Name]
[Address]
[Phone]
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.103 (30-day deadline), Sec. 92.104 (itemization requirement)

## 2. Notice to Repair or Remedy (Sec. 92.056)

**When to use**: Tenant providing written notice to landlord of condition affecting health/safety

**Requirements**:
- Written notice
- Specify problem/condition
- Specify building/portion affected
- Specify remedial action requested
- Proper delivery method (certified mail, hand delivery with receipt, or electronic if agreed)

---

### Template: Tenant Notice of Needed Repair

```
[Date]

[Landlord Name]
[Landlord Address]
[City, State ZIP]

SENT VIA: [Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested / Hand Delivery / Electronic Delivery]

RE: Notice of Needed Repair - [Property Address]

Dear [Landlord Name]:

This letter serves as formal written notice pursuant to Texas Property Code Section 92.056 of a condition requiring repair at the property located at [Property Address].

PROBLEM/CONDITION:
[Detailed description of the problem, e.g., "The air conditioning unit is not functioning, and the indoor temperature consistently exceeds 85 degrees"]

AFFECTED AREA:
[Specify the building or portion affected, e.g., "Entire dwelling unit" or "Master bedroom and living room"]

REQUESTED REMEDIAL ACTION:
[Describe what you want done, e.g., "Repair or replace the air conditioning unit to restore cooling function"]

DATE PROBLEM FIRST NOTICED: [Date]

This condition materially affects the physical health and safety of an ordinary tenant. I request that you remedy this condition as soon as reasonably possible.

Please contact me at [Phone] or [Email] to schedule access for inspection and repair.

Sincerely,

[Tenant Signature]
[Tenant Printed Name]
[Property Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

CC: [If applicable, copy to property manager]
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.056 (tenant notice requirements for repair remedies)

## 3. Landlord Delay Affidavit (Sec. 92.0562)

**When to use**: When landlord cannot complete repairs within reasonable time due to delay beyond landlord's control

**Requirements**:
- Sworn affidavit
- State general nature and date of casualty/contagion
- State reasonable time for remedying  
- State cause of delay beyond landlord's control

---

### Template: Affidavit for Delay in Repair

```
STATE OF TEXAS        §
                      §
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]    §

AFFIDAVIT FOR DELAY IN REPAIR
(Texas Property Code Sec. 92.0562)

I, [Landlord Name], being duly sworn, state as follows:

1. I am the landlord/authorized agent for the property located at [Property Address] leased to [Tenant Name].

2. On [Date], the tenant provided written notice of the following condition requiring repair: [Description of condition].

3. A casualty occurred on or about [Date of casualty]. The general nature of the casualty was: [e.g., "Severe winter storm causing widespread freezing and pipe bursts"].

OR

An infectious disease contagion occurred on or about [Date]. The general nature of the contagion was: [Description].

4. A reasonable period of time for remedying the condition is [X] days from the date of the tenant's notice.

5. The delay in remedying the condition is beyond my control due to the following cause(s):
   [Examples: "Unavailability of materials due to supply chain disruption caused by the storm" or "Unavailability of licensed contractors due to high demand following widespread storm damage" or "Quarantine requirements preventing contractor access"]

6. I am making good faith efforts to remedy the condition as quickly as reasonably possible under the circumstances.

FURTHER AFFIANT SAYETH NOT.

                              ________________________________
                              [Landlord Signature]
                              [Landlord Printed Name]

SWORN TO AND SUBSCRIBED before me on this ____ day of __________, 20___.

                              ________________________________
                              Notary Public, State of Texas
                              My Commission Expires: __________
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.0562 (extension of reasonable repair time for delays beyond landlord's control)

## 4. Notice of Intent to Terminate for Repair Issues (Sec. 92.056)

**When to use**: Tenant electing to terminate lease after landlord fails to repair within reasonable time

---

### Template: Notice of Lease Termination Due to Repair Failure

```
[Date]

[Landlord Name]
[Landlord Address]  
[City, State ZIP]

SENT VIA: [Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested]

RE: Notice of Lease Termination - [Property Address]

Dear [Landlord Name]:

On [Date of original notice], I provided you with written notice of a condition materially affecting the physical health and safety of an ordinary tenant at [Property Address], as required by Texas Property Code Section 92.056.

The condition described was: [Brief description of condition].

As of the date of this letter, a reasonable time has passed and you have not remedied the condition. Pursuant to my rights under Texas Property Code Section 92.056(e), I am exercising my right to terminate the lease.

This notice constitutes my termination of the lease agreement. I will vacate the premises on [Date - must be reasonable, typically 7-30 days], at which time I expect the return of my security deposit in accordance with Texas Property Code Section 92.103.

I will be available at [Phone] and [Email] to coordinate the move-out inspection and key return.

Sincerely,

[Tenant Signature]
[Tenant Printed Name]
[Current Address]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.056(e) (tenant's right to terminate for failure to repair)

## 5. Family Violence Lease Termination Notice (Sec. 92.016)

**When to use**: Tenant who is family violence victim terminating lease early

**Requirements**:
- Written notice
- Copy of protective order OR documentation specified in statute
- Move-out by later of: 30 days after notice OR end of month following notice month

---

### Template: Lease Termination - Family Violence

```
[Date]

[Landlord Name]
[Landlord Address]
[City, State ZIP]

SENT VIA: [Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested / Hand Delivery]

RE: Notice of Lease Termination Due to Family Violence - [Property Address]

Dear [Landlord Name]:

Pursuant to Texas Property Code Section 92.016, I am providing written notice of my intent to terminate the lease for the property located at [Property Address].

I am a victim of family violence as defined by Texas Family Code Section 71.004, and I have attached a copy of [protective order / official documentation of family violence protective order status / dating violence protective order] as required by Section 92.016(b).

In accordance with Section 92.016(e), I will surrender possession of the dwelling on or before [Date - must be later of: 30 days from this notice OR last day of the month following the month of this notice].

Pursuant to Section 92.016(f), I request that information regarding my early termination not be disclosed except as required by law or judicial order.

Please contact me at [Phone - may be new number if safety concern] to coordinate the move-out process.

Sincerely,

[Tenant Signature]
[Tenant Printed Name]

ATTACHMENT: [Copy of protective order or other qualifying documentation]
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.016 (early lease termination for family violence victims)

**IMPORTANT NOTES**:
- Similar templates exist for sexual assault victims (Sec. 92.0161) and stalking victims (Sec. 92.0162)
- Documentation requirements vary by offense type
- Landlord must maintain confidentiality

## 6. Request for Lock Change (Sec. 92.156, 92.159, 92.164)

**When to use**: Tenant requesting landlord rekey locks

---

### Template: Request to Rekey Locks

```
[Date]

[Landlord Name]
[Landlord Address]
[City, State ZIP]

RE: Request to Rekey Locks - [Property Address]

Dear [Landlord Name]:

Pursuant to Texas Property Code Section [92.156 / 92.159 / 92.164], I am requesting that you rekey the locks at [Property Address] for the following reason:

[SELECT APPLICABLE REASON:]

☐ A previous occupant or other person who might have a key has moved out, and I have not given that person permission to re-enter. (Sec. 92.156 - Tenant pays)

☐ I am a victim of family violence as defined in Texas Family Code Section 71.004, and I have attached [copy of protective order / police report or incident report documenting family violence]. (Sec. 92.159 - Landlord pays)

☐ I am a victim of sexual assault, sexual abuse, indecency with a child, stalking, or trafficking and I have attached [copy of protective order / conviction records / deferred adjudication records]. (Sec. 92.164 - Landlord pays)

☐ I have reasonable grounds to believe an unauthorized person has a key, based on: [Describe evidence of unauthorized entry]. (Sec. 92.156(e) - Tenant pays unless caused by landlord's fault)

Texas Property Code Section [92.156 / 92.159 / 92.164] requires that you rekey the locks within seven (7) days of receiving this request.

[If tenant pays]: I understand I am responsible for the reasonable cost of rekeying.

[If landlord pays]: Pursuant to Section [92.159 / 92.164], you are required to bear the cost of rekeying.

Please contact me at [Phone] or [Email] to schedule the rekeying.

Sincerely,

[Tenant Signature]
[Tenant Printed Name]
[Property Address]
[Phone]
[Email]

[If applicable]: ATTACHMENT: [Documentation required by statute]
```

**Legal basis**: 
- Texas Property Code Sec. 92.156 (general rekeying requests)
- Texas Property Code Sec. 92.159 (family violence victims)
- Texas Property Code Sec. 92.164 (sexual assault, abuse, stalking, trafficking victims)

## 7. Disclosure of Property Ownership (Sec. 92.201)

**When to use**: In response to tenant request OR at lease signing if tenant requests

**Requirements**: Provide within 7 days of request

---

### Template: Ownership Disclosure

```
[Date]

[Tenant Name]
[Property Address]
[City, State ZIP]

RE: Disclosure of Property Ownership - [Property Address]

Dear [Tenant Name]:

Pursuant to Texas Property Code Section 92.201, this letter provides the required disclosure of ownership and management for the property located at [Property Address].

PROPERTY OWNER:
Name: [Owner's full legal name or entity name]
Business Street Address: [No P.O. Box - must be street address]
City, State ZIP: [City, State ZIP]

MANAGEMENT COMPANY (if applicable):
Name: [Management company name]
Business Street Address: [Street address]
City, State ZIP: [City, State ZIP]
Phone: [Phone number]

If you have any questions, please contact [Management company/Owner] at [Phone number].

Sincerely,

[Landlord/Property Manager Name]
[Title]
```

**Legal basis**: Texas Property Code Sec. 92.201 (disclosure of ownership and management)

## 9. Notice of Intention to Enter (Not Required by Statute but Best Practice)

**When to use**: Before non-emergency entry

**Note**: Texas Property Code does not specify landlord entry notice requirements, but providing notice is a best practice

---

### Template: Notice of Entry

```
[Date]

[Tenant Name]
[Property Address]
[City, State ZIP]

RE: Notice of Entry - [Property Address]

Dear [Tenant Name]:

This letter provides notice that [Landlord/Property Manager/Maintenance Staff] will need to enter the property located at [Property Address] for the following purpose:

Reason for Entry: [e.g., "Conduct routine maintenance inspection" / "Repair reported plumbing issue" / "Show property to prospective tenant" / etc.]

Date of Entry: [Date]
Approximate Time: [Time range, e.g., "between 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM"]

[If applicable]: Please ensure that the following is accessible: [e.g., "Water heater closet," "Attic access," etc.]

If this date/time is not convenient, please contact me immediately at [Phone] to reschedule.

Sincerely,

[Landlord/Property Manager Name]
[Company]
[Phone]
```

**Legal basis**: While not required by statute, providing notice supports landlord's obligation not to abuse access rights per Sec. 92.008

## General Delivery Methods for Notices

Per Texas Property Code, various sections specify delivery methods. Common acceptable methods include:

1. **Certified or Registered Mail, Return Receipt Requested**
2. **Hand Delivery** with signed written receipt
3. **Electronic Delivery** if parties agreed in lease to electronic delivery method

Always keep proof of delivery.

## Additional Notice Types Covered by Statute

The following situations also have notice requirements under Chapter 92:

- **Casualty loss** (Sec. 92.054)
- **Landlord's intent to close premises** (Sec. 92.055)
- **Smoke detector issues** (Sec. 92.253-92.260)
- **Utility interruption for repairs** (Sec. 92.008)
- **Vehicle towing** (Sec. 92.0131)
- **Flood plain disclosure** (Sec. 92.0135)

Consult the full text in texas_property_code_chapter_92.txt and seek legal counsel for templates on these situations.
